# Topical Authority Framework
## Source: Noel Ceta (@noelcetaSEO) — Built authority from zero, ranked above DR 70+ sites in 6 months

## The Core Insight

Domain Authority and backlinks are NOT prerequisites for ranking.
A new site with zero backlinks and no existing authority can outrank high-DA competitors by building deep topical authority first.

The mechanism: Google's quality raters and AI systems evaluate topical expertise as a signal of E-E-A-T. A site that comprehensively covers a topic across all its angles signals genuine expertise — not just links.

## The Framework

### Phase 1: Pick One Topic Cluster (Week 1-2)

Choose the narrowest possible topic you can own completely.
- Not "retail software" — too broad
- Not "inventory management for jewelry stores" — too narrow to sustain content
- **Right size:** "Inventory decision intelligence for independent multi-location retailers"

The cluster should be:
- Specific enough that a high-DA generalist site won't bother competing
- Broad enough to sustain 8-15 pieces of content
- Aligned with your actual product/service

### Phase 2: Map Every Sub-Topic (Week 2-3)

For your chosen cluster, map every question, sub-topic, and related concept a searcher might encounter.

Example for "independent retailer inventory decisions":
- What is safety stock and how do you calculate it?
- How do you know when to reorder?
- What is landed cost?
- What POS data is actually useful?
- How do you identify slow movers?
- What is a reorder point?
- How do you read a POS inventory report?
- What metrics matter for inventory health?
- etc.

### Phase 3: Create Pillar + Spoke Content (Week 3-12)

**Pillar page:** One comprehensive resource covering the entire topic at a high level. 2000-3000 words.
- Example: "The Complete Guide to Inventory Decisions for Independent Retailers"

**Spoke pages:** Individual deep-dives on each sub-topic. 800-1200 words each.
- Each spoke links to the pillar and to 2-3 related spokes
- Pillar links to all spokes

### Phase 4: Internal Linking (Ongoing)

The internal link structure signals to Google that your site is an authoritative cluster, not a collection of isolated pages.

Pattern:
- Pillar → all spokes (hub-and-spoke)
- Spoke → related spokes (contextual)
- All pages → parent topic pages

### Phase 5: Earn Natural Links (Month 4-6)

Once you have genuine topical depth, links come naturally:
- Other sites cite your pillar as a source
- Bloggers link to your spokes when covering related topics
- You become the reference answer for your niche

## Why This Works for Coodra

Coodra's natural topic cluster: "retail inventory decision intelligence for independent retailers"

This is narrow enough that Netstock and Cin7 don't fully own it (they target mid-market/enterprise). It's broad enough to sustain 15-20 content pieces.

**The competitive advantage:** Netstock writes for companies with ERPs. Coodra writes for retailers running Shopify, Square, or Lightspeed with no ERP. That IS the niche.

**Existing content already started this:**
- "5 inventory mistakes" — one spoke
- "Demand forecasting without an ERP" — one spoke
- Integrations page — supporting content
- Comparison page (when built) — supporting content

3. Case studies as evidence (links from spokes to case studies)

## Content Quality Rules for Topical Authority

1. Each piece must add genuine depth — not rephrased surface-level content
2. Specific examples and data points > generic advice
3. Original frameworks or mental models > borrowed frameworks
4. Practical how-to > theory
5. Internal linking to related spokes is not optional — it's the mechanism

## Common Mistakes

- **Too broad too fast:** Trying to cover "retail software" instead of "inventory decisions for Shopify retailers"
- **No pillar:** Publishing spokes without a hub means no page accumulates enough authority
- **No internal linking:** Isolated pages don't build cluster authority
- **Chasing backlinks before authority:** Build the cluster first, then earn links naturally
