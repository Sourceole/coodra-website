# Link Building for B2B SaaS
## From practitioner case studies

## Why Links Still Matter

Links remain the #1 Google ranking factor. Without authoritative linking domains, content rarely ranks competitively. For B2B SaaS, link building is harder than e-commerce or publishing — you can't rely on news coverage or viral content.

## Effective B2B SaaS Link Building Tactics

### 1. Original Research / Data
**What:** Publish unique industry data no competitor has
**Why it works:** Journalists, bloggers, and researchers link to data as a source
**For coodra:** Annual "State of Independent Retail Inventory Decisions" report — survey retailers, publish findings, promote to trade publications

### 2. Expert Positioning
**What:** Get founder/team quoted in industry publications
**Why it works:** Publications link to credible sources; you get a mention and link
**Process:** Identify journalists covering retail technology, reach out with expert commentary on stories they're covering

### 3. Resource Page Outreach
**What:** Get listed on "tools for retailers" or "best inventory management software" pages
**Why it works:** Resource pages exist specifically to link to useful tools
**Process:** Find resource pages via: "best [category] tools" OR "[category] resources" search queries, pitch Coodra as a valuable addition

### 4. Competitor Backlink Replication
**What:** Find where competitors are linked, then pitch yourself as an alternative
**Why it works:** If a site links to Netstock, they might link to Coodra too
**Process:** Use backlink analysis to find competitor links, identify sites that would logically cover both, reach out with Coodra value prop

### 5. Integration Partner Links
**What:** Get links from Shopify App Store, Square App Marketplace, Lightspeed partner directories
**Why it works:** Partner directories are designed to link to integrations
**For coodra:** Already integrated — ensure Coodra is listed in each partner directory with a profile link

### 6. Comparison Content Links
**What:** Create genuinely useful comparison content that other sites reference
**Why it works:** Comparison pages get linked to when sites need to cite alternatives
**For coodra:** The comparison page itself — become the page other sites cite when comparing inventory tools

## Link Quality Over Quantity

### High-Quality Links Characteristics
- From relevant, authoritative sites in retail/technology
- Editorial context (link within relevant content, not sidebar/footer)
- Diverse anchor text (not over-optimized)
- Traffic-driving (links from sites users actually visit)
- Growing over time (consistent link accruement rate)

### Red Flags
- PBN (Private Blog Network) links — Google detects and penalizes
- Paid links without nofollow — violates guidelines
- Irrelevant directory submissions — low value
- Comment spam / forum spam — harmful
- Links from link farms or link exchanges — detectable

## Measuring Link Health

### Metrics to Track
- Total referring domains (growing = healthy)
- Domain Authority / DR of referring domains
- Link accruement rate month-over-month
- Lost links (decay monitoring)
- Anchor text distribution (natural diversity)

### When to Act
- Referring domains declining 3+ months
- Sudden link loss (check for technical issues or removals)
- Anchor text suddenly over-optimized
- New links from suspicious sources (disavow if PBN suspected)
