# Content Strategy Reference

## Source: Nick Zviadadze (Nick_zv_) — 366K impressions in 3 months case study

## The Data-First Content Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Source Data
Priority order:
1. **Google Search Console** — non-branded queries, click gaps, impression opportunities
2. **Competitor analysis** — what keywords competitors rank for, content gaps
3. **Industry reports** — government data, trade associations, market research
4. **Community forums** — Reddit threads, Quora, industry-specific communities
5. **Reviews** — what users say about existing solutions (App Store, G2, Trustpilot)

### Step 2: Pattern Recognition
From raw data, identify:
- **Content angles** that appear repeatedly
- **Questions people actually ask** (extracted from queries, not assumed)
- **What Google signals as desired coverage** (query intent analysis)
- **What competitors cover poorly** (gaps to exploit)

### Step 3: Content That Satisfies Intent
Rules:
- Answer the primary query in the first 100 words
- Go beyond the stated query — answer the unstated question
- Include actionable takeaways, not just information
- Structure with clear heading hierarchy matching query structure
- Link to related internal pages naturally

### Step 4: AI Overview Optimization
Triggers for AI Overviews appearing:
- Use numbered and bulleted lists
- Use tables for comparisons
- FAQ format with direct Q&A
- Complete answers, not partial
- Demonstrated expertise (specific facts, not generic definitions)
- Cite authoritative sources

### Step 5: Topic Clusters
Build authority through related content:
- One comprehensive pillar page per core topic
- 3-5 supporting cluster pages per pillar
- Internal links from cluster → pillar
- Pillar → cluster cross-links

### Step 6: Link Building for B2B SaaS
Effective tactics:
- **Digital PR** — publish original data or research
- **Expert positioning** — founder quotes in industry publications
- **Resource page outreach** — get listed on "tools for retailers" pages
- **Competitor backlink replication** — find competitor links, pitch Coodra as alternative
- **Partnership content** — co-create with complementary (non-competing) tools

### Step 7: Technical Foundation
Non-negotiable per-page requirements:
- LCP < 2.5s
- Mobile responsive
- Clean URL structure (descriptive, hyphenated)
- Schema markup for content type
- HTTPS enforced

## Content Quality Checklist

### Must Have
- [ ] Primary query answered in first 100 words
- [ ] Clear heading hierarchy (H2s/H3s)
- [ ] Internal links to 2+ related pages
- [ ] CTA aligned with content intent
- [ ] Publication date visible
- [ ] Author name and title visible

### Should Have
- [ ] FAQ section (natural extension of content)
- [ ] Original data, statistics, or specific examples
- [ ] External links to authoritative sources
- [ ] Table or list format for complex information
- [ ] Related content suggestions at bottom

### Nice to Have
- [ ] Video or embedded media
- [ ] Downloadable resource (guide, checklist, template)
- [ ] Author bio with credentials
- [ ] Last updated date (for time-sensitive topics)

## For Coodra Specifically

### High-Value Content Types
1. **Comparison guides** — Coodra vs alternatives (comparison pages)
2. **How-to articles** — specific retail inventory problems
3. **Calculators/tools** — interactive ROI estimators
4. **Industry research** — "State of Independent Retail Inventory 2026"
5. **Case studies (short)** — one specific win, specific numbers

### Content Gaps to Fill
- "How to calculate safety stock for [retail type]"
- "Signs your inventory reorder point is wrong"
- "What is landed cost and how to calculate it"
- "How to read your POS inventory report"
- "Inventory turnover benchmarks by retail category"

### Blog Post Length Guidelines
- Informational/query-answering: 800-1200 words
- Comprehensive guides: 1500-2500 words
- Data-driven/research posts: 2000-3000 words
- Product comparisons: 1200-1800 words

Note: Word count is not a ranking factor. Quality and completeness matter. A 400-word post that fully answers a query beats a 2000-word post that doesn't.
